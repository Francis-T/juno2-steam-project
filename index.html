STEAM: Secure and Trustworthy Framework for Integrated Energy and Mobility in Smart Connected Communities

This collaborative project between US and Japan, called STEAM, aims to develop novel integrated frameworks to address important security and trustworthiness challenges in two emerging Smart Connected Community (SCC) applications -- smart mobility and energy.

In particular, considering various threat models, we propose innovative solutions to five inter-related research thrusts:

  1. Secure and trustworthy decision making under uncertainty, by developing efficient anomaly detection and trust models;
  2. Integrating privacy-preserving computations with secure and trustworthy decision making schemes by adopting fully homomorphic encryption (FHE);
  3. Characterizing trade-offs between security, privacy and available resources;
  4. Developing a secure and trustworthy middleware architecture as applied to smart mobility and energy, that incorporates privacy- preserving algorithms, resource constraints, and trustworthiness of data sources and decision making schemes; and
  5. Evaluating the architecture and proposed solutions with exemplar case study applications.

Research Thrusts

Secure and Trustworthy Decision Making under Uncertainty

How do we ensure secure and trustworthy decisions across integrated domains of smart energy and mobility networks? How do we make robust decisions for end customers in SCC using network data that could be manipulated, omitted, or delayed by adversarial behavior?

To this end, we propose novel light-weight anomaly detection algorithms, and stochastic trust and robust decision models to minimuze uncertainty and risks.

Privacy-preserving Computations using FHE

Given that anomaly detection requirese contextual information, such as smart meter data for energy pricing bids (used by the utility for high fidelity energy control) or smart vehicle trajectories, how do we ensure computations and analytics that also preserve the privacy of end customers?

We adopt efficient Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) algorithms for anomaly detection and trustworthy decision making at the community scale.

Characterizing Security, Privacy, and Resource Trade-offs

Given that FHE is computationally slow and resource intensive, how do we ensure efficient security and privacy for resource-constrained SCC applications deploying IoT and edge computing devices?

We define metrics for analyzing the trade-offs between resource utilization and timeliness (or responsiveness) vs. security, privacy, and trustworthiness in SCC decision support systems.

Developing Secure and Trustworthy Middleware Architecture

How do we build multi-domain architecture for design, analysis, and deployment of integrated smart mobility and energy solutions? How and where in the network do we implement computations related to privacy, security, and trust? What are the computational and resource challenges in task delegation for scalability?

We propose a novel middleware framework that distributes security features across tasks and incorporates privacy, trustworthiness, resource constraints, and distributed decision support.

Validation with Exemplar SCC Applications

How do we evaluate proposed models, solutions, and resource-provisioning architecture for trustworthy computations and control?

We validate smart mobility and energy distribution scenarios with simulation studies.
